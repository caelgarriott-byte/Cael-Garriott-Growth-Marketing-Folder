# Campaign Template — Generic Nonprofit

Copy this structure for each active program your nonprofit runs. Every campaign must meet Google's structural minimums or it won't pass the final review.

**Structural minimums (non-negotiable):**
- ≥2 ad groups per campaign
- ≥2 responsive search ads (RSAs) per ad group
- ≥2 sitelink extensions at the account level
- All keywords must be mission-relevant (no single-word keywords)
- No keywords with Quality Score below 3

---

## Campaign settings (apply to every campaign)

| Setting | Value |
|---|---|
| Campaign type | Search only |
| Search partners | OFF |
| Display expansion | OFF |
| Location targeting | Where you actually serve people ("Presence" — not "Presence or interest") |
| Language | English (add others if relevant) |
| Bidding | Maximize Conversions (if tracking live) / Maximize Clicks with $2 cap (if not) |
| Budget | $329/day (the grant max — set this and let Google spend what it can) |

---

## Campaign structure template

```
Campaign: [Program Name]
│
├── Ad Group A: [Primary search intent — e.g., "life skills programs"]
│   ├── Keywords (5–10, phrase/exact match)
│   ├── RSA 1
│   └── RSA 2
│
└── Ad Group B: [Secondary search intent — e.g., "autism programs adults"]
    ├── Keywords (5–10, phrase/exact match)
    ├── RSA 1
    └── RSA 2
```

---

## Responsive Search Ad (RSA) template

Google assembles RSAs from your headlines and descriptions — provide as many as possible (up to 15 headlines, 4 descriptions) and let Google find the best combinations.

**Headlines (max 30 chars each):**
- [Program name] at [Org name]
- [Who you serve — e.g., "For Neurodivergent Adults"]
- [Location — e.g., "South Bay Los Angeles"]
- [Benefit — e.g., "Build Real Independence"]
- [CTA — e.g., "Learn More Today"]
- [Differentiator — e.g., "501(c)(3) Nonprofit"]
- [Specificity — e.g., "Ages 18–30"]

**Descriptions (max 90 chars each):**
- [What the program does in one sentence.] [Location + audience.]
- [Outcome or benefit.] [CTA — "Reach out to learn more" / "See upcoming sessions."]

**Final URL:** Point to the specific program page, not the homepage.

---

## Ad group keyword strategy

- Use **phrase match** `"like this"` and **exact match** `[like this]` — avoid broad match in Ad Grants accounts (it wastes budget on irrelevant traffic and kills CTR)
- 5–10 keywords per ad group is enough — quality over quantity
- Every keyword must be tied to your mission and the specific program in that ad group
- Check the search terms report weekly and add irrelevant terms to your negative keyword list

---

## Account-level extensions (required)

**Sitelinks (add at least 2, ideally 4+):**
- Link to each program page
- Link to your contact/apply page
- Link to your about page

**Callouts (optional but recommended):**
- 501(c)(3) Nonprofit
- [Your location]
- [Who you serve]
- [Key differentiator]

**Structured snippets:**
- Header: "Programs"
- Values: list your active programs

---

## Coming-soon programs

If you have programs not yet launched, run a lightweight waitlist campaign:
- Same structure (2 ad groups, 2 RSAs each)
- Headlines focus on "Coming Soon" and "Join the Waitlist"
- Final URL points to a waitlist signup form or contact page
- This builds a warm audience before launch and keeps the account active
