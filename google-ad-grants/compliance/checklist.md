# Monthly Compliance Checklist

Run this once a month. It takes 15–20 minutes and keeps the grant from being suspended. Two months of non-compliance on any of these triggers automatic suspension.

---

## The rules (ranked by how often they kill accounts)

1. **5% CTR minimum, every month** — below 5% for two consecutive months = automatic suspension
2. **Conversion tracking must exist and fire** — at least one meaningful conversion tracked
3. **Quality Score ≥ 3** — keywords with QS 1–2 must be paused or removed
4. **Structure minimums** — ≥2 ad groups/campaign, ≥2 ads/ad group, ≥2 sitelinks at account level
5. **No single-word keywords** — everything must be mission-relevant
6. **Geo-targeting required** — must reflect your real service area
7. **$2 max CPC** — unless using Maximize Conversions / Target CPA / Target ROAS
8. **Search only** — no Display, no Search partners
9. **Annual program survey** — Google sends a survey each year to keep the grant; complete it when it arrives or the account gets deactivated
10. **Active management** — accounts must be logged into and managed; dormant accounts get paused

---

## The non-negotiables

### ✅ CTR ≥ 5% (most important)

Where to check: Campaigns → set date range to last 30 days → check CTR column.

If CTR is below 5%:
- Pause keywords with low impressions and 0 clicks
- Pause keywords with many impressions but low CTR (they're dragging the average down)
- Add more specific, higher-intent keywords
- Improve ad relevance — make sure headlines match what people are searching
- Add negative keywords to filter irrelevant traffic

**Don't panic if one ad group is low** — what matters is the account-level CTR. But dig into which campaigns or ad groups are dragging it down.

### ✅ Conversion tracking is active

Where to check: Goals → Conversions → confirm the conversion action shows recent data and status is "Active."

If it shows "No recent conversions" or "Inactive":
- Check that the tag is still on the site (use Google Tag Assistant Chrome extension)
- Check that the conversion event is still firing
- Re-verify the GA4 link if using imported conversions
- After any site update, verify the tag is still firing correctly

### ✅ No keywords with Quality Score below 3

Where to check: Keywords tab → add the Quality Score column.

If QS < 3: Pause the keyword. Don't delete — pausing preserves the data.

### ✅ Every ad group has ≥ 2 active ads

Where to check: Ad groups tab → click into each group → check Ads tab.

If any group has only 1 active ad: Add a second RSA or unpause a paused one.

### ✅ Account has ≥ 2 sitelink extensions

Where to check: Campaigns → Assets → Sitelinks.

If missing: Add sitelinks at the account level pointing to key program pages.

### ✅ No single-word keywords active

Where to check: Keywords tab → sort by keyword → look for any single word.

If found: Pause them immediately. They violate Ad Grant policy.

## Monthly optimizations (do these alongside compliance)

**Search terms report (10 min)**
- Campaigns → Insights & Reports → Search Terms
- Add irrelevant terms to your negative keyword list
- Add high-performing new terms as keywords

**Keyword performance**
- Pause keywords with 0 clicks in 90+ days
- Identify top-performing keywords and make sure their ad groups have strong, relevant ads

**Ad performance**
- Check which RSA headline/description combinations are getting the most impressions
- If an RSA has "Poor" ad strength, add more headline/description variety

**Budget utilization**
- Are you spending toward the $329/day cap? If not, expand keywords or add new campaigns for programs you haven't covered yet

---

## If the account gets suspended

1. Check the suspension notice email — it will name the specific policy violation
2. Fix every instance of the violation (don't just fix one)
3. Submit a reinstatement request through the Google for Nonprofits portal
4. Reinstatement typically takes 5–10 business days

Common suspension reasons:
- CTR below 5% for two consecutive months
- No valid conversion action
- Single-word keywords
- Ads pointing to a broken or low-quality landing page
- Account inactive (no changes or logins in 90 days)

---

## Quarterly review

Every quarter, do a deeper pass:
- Are there new programs to build campaigns for?
- Are landing pages still relevant and high quality?
- Has the service area changed?
- Are there seasonal search trends to capitalize on (enrollment season, back to school, end of year)?
- Review impression share — if it's low, you may have budget left on the table
