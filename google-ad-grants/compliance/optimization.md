# Optimization — Getting More From the Grant

For when the account is healthy and you want more: more of the $10k spent, more clicks, more program inquiries.

---

## The reality of the $10k

Most Ad Grant accounts never spend the full $10,000/month, and that's normal — most nonprofits serve a local area with limited search volume. The goal isn't to max out spend for its own sake. It's to capture every relevant local searcher. Under-spend is fine. Spending on off-mission, non-local traffic to hit a number is not.

---

## Lever 1 — Conversion tracking unlocks everything (do this first)

If only one optimization happens, make it this. With reliable conversion tracking:

- The account can switch to **Maximize Conversions**, which **removes the $2 CPC cap** — so your ads can compete for valuable keywords that cost $4–8/click (the $2 cap silently shuts you out of these)
- Bidding optimizes toward actual inquiries, not just clicks
- It satisfies the compliance requirement at the same time

The conversion to track: a program inquiry — contact form submit or apply/sign-up button click. See `setup/conversion-tracking.md`.

---

## Lever 2 — Switch to Maximize Conversions bidding

Once conversion tracking has data (give it 2+ weeks of conversions), switch campaigns from Maximize Clicks to **Maximize Conversions**.

This is the single biggest performance unlock for an Ad Grant account. Watch CPCs after switching — they'll rise (that's the point), but conversion quality should improve significantly.

---

## Lever 3 — Quality Score → more visibility at no extra cost

Higher Quality Score = better ad position at the same bid. This matters doubly under the $2 cap.

To raise QS:
- **Tight ad group themes** — one narrow intent per ad group, keywords + ads + landing page all aligned
- **Keyword language on the landing page** — if the ad group targets "life skills program," the landing page should actually say "life skills program"
- **Strong RSAs** — use all headline/description slots, include a location, keep CTAs clear
- **Pause QS 1–2 keywords** — they drag down the whole account

---

## Lever 4 — Mine the Search Terms report monthly

Open Search Terms (Campaigns → Insights & Reports → Search Terms):

- **Winners** → add high-performing actual queries as new exact-match keywords
- **Losers** → add irrelevant queries to your negative keyword list

This single habit compounds over time. The account gets sharper every month.

---

## Lever 5 — Landing page quality

Ad performance is capped by where the clicks land. For each program campaign, the landing page should:
- Load fast
- State the program clearly above the fold
- Use the same language as the searcher's query
- Put the inquiry action (form / contact button) above the fold
- Include an FAQ section (helps Quality Score and conversion rate)

---

## Lever 6 — Expand campaigns as your programs grow

Once the core campaigns are running well, add campaigns for:
- New programs launching
- Waitlist campaigns for coming-soon programs (builds a warm audience before launch)
- A branded campaign (your org name + variations) — low cost, high conversion rate

---

## Lever 7 — Geo and schedule tuning

- Keep targeting tight to your actual service area — a smaller relevant audience beats a broad one for CTR and Quality Score
- If data shows conversions cluster at certain times, consider ad scheduling — but don't over-engineer this for a small local account

---

## What "good" looks like

- CTR comfortably above 5% (aim for 8%+)
- Conversion tracking firing with a steady trickle of program inquiries
- Spend rising over time as Maximize Conversions and tightened keywords let ads show more
- Each program campaign sending qualified local traffic to a clear, relevant landing page

---

## Monthly report metrics to pull

- Clicks
- CTR (must stay ≥5%)
- Grant spend utilized
- Conversions (program inquiries)
- Top 5 performing keywords
- Any new negatives added
